# AIPS-1 Recognised Jurisdiction List

The Recognised Jurisdiction List is the governance artefact that identifies the jurisdictions whose Insurance Supervisors satisfy the eligibility criteria in AIPS-1 §10 — IAIS membership, ICP-consistent licensing regime, public register of authorised insurers, and admission under the published governance procedure.

A jurisdiction's presence on the List is the precondition for any Issuer incorporated or authorised in that jurisdiction to issue conformant AIPS-1 Policy Certificates. The List does **not** constitute substantive endorsement of any particular insurer or product authorised within an admitted jurisdiction.

## Status

**Deferred to v0.2 (Q3 2026).** The initial List, the governance procedure for amendment, and the formal admission process are all subject to public comment during the v0.1 review period.

## Provisional working assumption

For v0.1 review purposes only — not yet adopted, not normative — the following IAIS member jurisdictions are likely candidates for inclusion in the initial List, on the basis that each operates a developed insurance market with publicly verifiable licensing and supervision under ICP-aligned frameworks:

This is **illustrative**, not exhaustive and not adopted. Inclusion criteria, additions, removals, and the governance procedure for the List are the substantive matters under consultation.

## Open questions for v0.1 comment

- Should the List be governed by AIPS-1's own governance body, or delegated to an existing standards organisation (most plausibly the IAIS)?
- What admission criteria should apply beyond IAIS membership and ICP consistency — e.g. enforcement track record, public-register quality, treatment of captives?
- What process should govern removal or suspension of a jurisdiction from the List?
- Should the List distinguish between Tier I / II / III eligibility per jurisdiction, or treat admission as binary?
- Should non-IAIS-member jurisdictions with substantively equivalent supervision be eligible for admission?
